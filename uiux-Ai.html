Project Overview

Project outline / Clients’ names / Project goals / Major deliverables

Objective:

Through Introductor, the AI Medical Referral Software, insurance agents can access health information to help policyholders choose the ideal hospital and coronary stents for surgery. As a result, insurance agents can develop strong and reliable relationships with policyholders to increase the likelihood of continued success in the insurance agent business.

Overview:

Most patients will ask their insurance agent for accurate and up-to-date information about their health insurance coverage before possible surgery as they need to estimate a budget for medical expenses, emergency funds, and more.

About 90 per cent of policyholders call an insurance agent for accurate and up-to-date information about their health insurance coverage after a possible surgery. Moreover, more than half of policyholders asked insurance agents for advice on more alternative information about choosing a hospital or surgery centre. However, about half of the insurance agents were not reluctant to advise because almost no policyholders had the medical knowledge to plan the surgery. In addition, people judge the quality of hospitals by different criteria. Advising policyholders on hospital or surgery can lead insurance agents into ethical dilemmas.


To address this question, we conducted interviews with insurance agents to observe pain points and scenarios. Insurance agents sincerely want to help clients choose the right medical speciality. They often seek guidance from colleagues and browse official government and hospital websites for the latest data and insights on choosing hospitals and specialists. However, due to the below reasons:

  • Difficult to schedule consultations with the best professionals
  • Health information can find online is often fragmented, incomplete, complicated and jargon-filled.
    • The insurance agents felt dazzled, difficult to find out the data they were looking for and the entire process is time-consuming and inefficien.

    Thus, this software is designed to assist insurance agents to help their customer service in finding hospitals and coronary stents. In addition, the team consulted with healthcare professionals for their insights to build software with objective criteria for evaluating quality hospitals and coronary stents. Additionally, to help insurance agents easily understand medical information,

  • Statistical Graphics
  • Charts
  • Infographics
    • Are used in the software to explain the strengths and weaknesses of hospitals and surgeons.

    Insurance agents say the software provides understandable judgment to help their clients make thoughtful decisions. Furthermore, during doctor consultations, patients also use data from the software to discuss the outcome of possible surgery.

    Starting the Design

    Include problems, solutions, flowcharts, wireframes to understand the beginning of a design

    Problem:

    Design a simple and direct user interface that communicates useful healthcare information to insurance agents, enabling insurance agents to help their clients gain objective insights when choosing the ideal surgeon and hospital. By doing so, policyholders can make informed decisions.

    Almost insurance agents mentioned that the best solution is to help policyholders make informed decisions during planned surgery, rather than providing a list of surgeons. With this in mind, we interviewed 9 insurance agents and 7 policyholders to gain more constructive insights into the decision-making and planning process for surgery. The observations are as follows:

    Solutions:

    To help insurance agents (primary users) provide policyholders with excellent customer service (finding ideal surgery centres and surgeons), the software is equipped with the following solutions to meet policyholders' needs.

    A way to search according to their priority:

    People have different priorities in the criteria for choosing a hospital and a surgeon. To solve this problem, users can find the information they want based on their needs. Users can start by finding an authoritative doctor in the field, or set search criteria based on location.

    Users Want A Database of Physicians:

    Respondents expressed difficulty in arranging a consultation with an authoritative physician, even a disciple or partner of an authoritative physician. The software analyzes the authoritative physician’s connections based on her/his doctoral dissertation writing, surgical collaboration, graduation school, etc. Providing the authoritative physician’s database, the policyholder can make an appointment with her/his disciple or cooperative doctor.

    Users Want All Information in One Place:

    Almost medical-related data is scattered across different websites, and users want an aggregated presentation that includes everything a policyholder needs during a planned surgery.

    Data Needs to Be Organized and Defined:

    To help users easily access and understand the meaning of the data, data was sorted based on the priority to comprehend the story the data is telling.

    Visualize to Illustrate Data:

    To communicate medically relevant information clearly and effectively, we use statistical graphics, charts, and infographics to convey quantitative information visually.

    Flowchart:

    I made a flowchart based on these needs, but the first version was too complicated because the users of this software are insurance agents. It's better to let them do their tasks in one place. For example, they can also apply for compensation and submit medical bill receipts here. However, doing so requires passing data and commands back between our database and the insurance company's database. This can be beneficial to users, as they can complete tasks quickly, but adds cost, workflow, and organizational complexity. Finally, to simplify the process, I divided the flowchart into three main steps, login, search and get results.

    Wireframe:

    When the wireframing started, I started guiding the assisted user to find a solution, but after testing, the user responded that they had to choose a number of healthcare quality metrics. They knew it was important and the medical professionals used it to make their decisions, but not many people cared about it, so I changed it and started using a diversity search method where users start looking for top doctors by typing in their names of peers or search results by location.

    Then, the user also mentioned that we only provide a way to export search results, but insurance agents have to serve people of different ages. Some seniors are unfamiliar with the Line and What's apps to access files. They still prefer to read articles and statistics on paper, which also makes the information feel more reliable. Therefore, I provide multiple ways to deliver results and allow policyholders to receive documents via email and print them out.

    Refining the design

    Utilize visual design to help users access and understand user interfaces

    UI Design:

    To enable users to have a more cohesive user experience, experience a consistent design language and facilitate the smooth completion of the task at hand, we created a design system. Design systems have gradually increased the consistency and speed of software development.

    To develop the design system, firstly, rules and principles were established. Secondly, a colour palette was finalized to determine the primary colour and secondary colour. Then, typographic scale, icon libraries, and other styling properties such as padding and margins are standardized to eliminate inconsistencies and improve maintenance and scalability by reducing design and technical debt.

    Colour:

    Since blue and green are often associated with trust, knowledge, and professionalism, we used blue and green to convince users that the product provides credibility and expert advice to those picking hospitals and specialists.

    Typography:

    To allow users to read text effortlessly, Calibri is adopted because of its readability and legibility in web and mobile user interfaces
