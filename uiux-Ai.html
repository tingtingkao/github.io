Project Overview

Project outline / Clients’ names / Project goals / Major deliverables

Objective:

Through Introductor, the AI Medical Referral Software, insurance agents can access health information to help policyholders choose the ideal hospital and coronary stents for surgery. As a result, insurance agents can develop strong and reliable relationships with policyholders to increase the likelihood of continued success in the insurance agent business.

Overview:

About 90 per cent of policyholders turn to insurance agents for accurate, updated health coverage details before surgery.

Beyond coverage, over half also seek advice on selecting hospitals or procedures. However, agents often hesitate due to ethical concerns and lack of medical knowledge, especially since hospital quality is judged subjectively.

To understand this dilemma, we interviewed agents and found they actively search for reliable sources, government sites, internal forums, and peers, but often encounter fragmented or jargon-heavy information. They struggle to locate clear, objective criteria for advising clients.


TIntroductor addresses this by offering structured medical data and professional insights, focusing on coronary stents and hospitals. The interface uses statistical charts, infographics, and visualized comparisons to help agents and clients understand key differences. The tool not only supports informed decisions but also facilitates better communication between doctors, patients, and agents.

Starting the Design

Include problems, solutions, flowcharts, wireframes to understand the beginning of a design

Problem:

How might we design a simple and direct interface that helps insurance agents guide their clients in choosing ideal surgeons and hospitals based on objective medical data?

Agents emphasized that they simply want to help the clients to make informed decisions for planned surgeries. With this in mind, we interviewed 9 agents and 7 policyholders to explore their decision-making process.

Key Insights & Solutions

Key Insights

  • People prioritize different factors (e.g., authority, location) when choosing surgeons.
  • Policyholders often rely on scattered online suggestions without clear quality standards.
  • Medical data is hard to interpret without context or visualization.
  • Insurance agents serve a wide demographic, including seniors unfamiliar with digital tools.

    • Solutions:

  • Priority-based search: Users can start from top doctors or filter by region.
  • Physician network: The system maps relationships between top doctors and their collaborators (based on academic papers, surgeries, institutions).
  • All-in-one platform: Centralizes essential surgery-related data, allowing agents to support clients efficiently.
  • Structured data: Information is sorted and explained for easier understanding.
  • Visual clarity: Key metrics are visualized through infographics and charts.
  • Flexible delivery: Users can export results, send via email, or print them for seniors who prefer physical documents.

    • Flowchart:

    The initial flow tried to include claims and reimbursements, but it adds cost, workflow, and organizational complexity. Therefore, the flowchart was restructured into three main steps: Login, Search, Get results.

    Wireframe:

    Early wireframes focused on guiding users through quality metrics, but tests showed they preferred simpler search flows: by name or location. I also added multiple result-sharing formats to meet users' diverse preferences.
